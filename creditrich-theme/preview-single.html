Best Aeroplan Credit Cards
in Canada for 2026

SR
Sarah Reynolds March 19, 2026
8 min read
Updated Mar 2026
Photo: Unsplash / Clay Banks

If you fly Air Canada even a handful of times a year, an Aeroplan credit card is one of the highest-value financial products available to Canadians. The right card can earn you a free business-class flight in under 12 months — but with a dozen options on the market, choosing the wrong one means leaving thousands of points on the table.

We analyzed every Aeroplan-earning card available in Canada, scoring them on welcome bonus value, earn rates, annual fee offset, travel perks and approval accessibility. Here's exactly what we found.

Quick take: The TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite is the best all-around card for most Canadians. If you spend heavily on groceries and gas, the CIBC Aeroplan Visa Infinite edges ahead. High spenders should look at the American Express Aeroplan Reserve.

Our Top Aeroplan Card Picks

After running the numbers on average Canadian spending patterns — roughly $2,400/month across groceries, gas, dining and travel — here's how the top cards stack up in year-one value.

Best Overall: TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite

The TD Aeroplan Visa Infinite has held the top spot for three consecutive years, and 2026 is no different. The welcome bonus alone — up to 50,000 Aeroplan points when you meet the minimum spend — is worth approximately $750 in Air Canada flights when redeemed strategically.

Earn Rates Breakdown

  • 1.5 Aeroplan points per $1 spent at grocery stores and gas stations
  • 1.5 points per $1 on Air Canada purchases (stackable with Aeroplan number)
  • 1 point per $1 on all other eligible purchases
  • Bonus points on Air Canada flights when you add your Aeroplan number at checkout

The card's $139 annual fee is easily offset by the included travel insurance package — emergency medical, trip cancellation, and delayed baggage coverage that would cost $200+ if purchased separately.

"I put my groceries, gas and Air Canada flights on this card and hit the welcome bonus in three months. The lounge access alone made the fee worthwhile on my first trip."

— Marcus T., GoRewards community member

Premium Pick: Amex Aeroplan Reserve

If your annual spend exceeds $60,000 or you fly Air Canada business class regularly, the American Express Aeroplan Reserve card pays for itself many times over. The $599 annual fee is steep, but the card delivers Maple Leaf Lounge access, a companion pass, and a 3× earn rate on all Air Canada purchases.

Key Perks at a Glance

  • Unlimited Maple Leaf Lounge access for cardholder and one guest
  • Annual companion pass (economy) on Air Canada flights
  • 3× points on Air Canada, 2× on dining and food delivery
  • Comprehensive travel insurance including $5M emergency medical
  • Priority check-in, boarding and baggage handling on Air Canada

Best No-Fee Option: CIBC Aeroplan Visa

Not everyone wants to pay an annual fee, and the CIBC Aeroplan Visa is the strongest no-fee Aeroplan card available. You won't earn points as fast, but the 10,000-point welcome bonus and 1× earn rate on all purchases make it a solid entry point — especially if you're building credit or testing the Aeroplan ecosystem before committing to a premium card.
